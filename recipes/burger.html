Burger

Description

A burger is a savory sandwich made with a cooked patty—usually beef—placed between two halves of a bun. It’s often topped with cheese, lettuce, tomato, onions, pickles, and condiments like ketchup or mayonnaise.

Burgers can be grilled, fried, or broiled, giving them a juicy and flavorful taste. They’re a popular meal choice worldwide, known for their satisfying combination of textures and flavors.

Ingredients

  • Ground beef
  • Potato Bun
  • Lettuce
  • Tomato
  • Onion
  • Cheese

Steps

  1. Prepare the patties – Season ground beef with salt and pepper, then shape it into round patties slightly larger than your buns.
  2. Cook the patties – Grill, pan-fry, or broil the patties for about 3–5 minutes per side, depending on your preferred doneness.
  3. Toast the buns – Lightly toast the burger buns on the grill or in a pan until golden.
  4. Assemble the burger – Place the cooked patty on the bottom bun, then add cheese, lettuce, tomato, onions, and any condiments you like.
  5. Serve and enjoy – Top with the other half of the bun and serve warm with fries or a side of your choice.
